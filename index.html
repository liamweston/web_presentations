A Day in the Life of Dr. Jane Mitchell

Why the future of web must start with the reality of the HCP

  • Jane is a consultant oncologist working in a high-pressure environment
  • Every decision has direct implications for patient outcomes
  • She does not have time to browse complex websites or navigate long PDFs
  • She needs fast, trusted, clinically relevant answers at the point of care
We start with the user reality. This is not a digital problem; it’s a clinical workflow problem

The Trigger Moment

A precise clinical question exposes the limits of today's web

Today

Jane is preparing for an MDT discussion. She asks: "Which therapies show the strongest progression-free survival benefit in second-line lung cancer?"

Multiple browser tabs open, fragmented sources, manual synthesis.

Future

She asks once and receives a structured, trusted, clinically grounded answer.

No manual synthesis. No fragmented sources. One question, one trusted response.

Today, Jane is acting as the integration layer. The future removes that burden

AI-Led Entry Point

Discovery starts where Jane already works

  • Jane asks her question in an AI assistant
  • Our organisation appears as a trusted, cited source
  • She lands directly on an intent-driven experience, not a homepage
  • The website becomes part of an AI-mediated discovery ecosystem
The web is no longer the starting point - it is one of many AI entry points

The Critical Risk: Context Collapse

Why traditional pharma governance models break

AI systems combine content across
  • Websites
  • Audiences
  • Content types
This creates "context collapse"
  • Promotional content may appear in medical contexts
  • Early science may be presented as established fact
  • Disclaimers and intended audience may be lost
Physical separation of websites is no longer sufficient. This is the burning platform.

Architecture

Design rules for a regulated, AI-enabled experience

Architectural Principles

Non-negotiable guardrails for the architecture

  • Structured content as the foundation
  • Separation of content control plane from delivery plane
  • Composable and modular
  • API-first and headless
  • AI-enabled but grounded
  • Compliance-by-design
  • Privacy-by-design
  • Clear separation of medical and promotional content
These are non-negotiable guardrails for the architecture
